Code

import json
import numpy

#i am defining a class to represent each student's data
class Student:
# this is a constructor with 4 arguments
    def __init__(self,name,grade, testscore, fav_subject):
        self.name= name
        self.grade = grade
        self.testscore = testscore
        self.fav_subject = fav_subject
#here i am reading the json data nd loading it into the json_data variable
with open('student.json') as student_json:
    json_data = json.load(student_json)



#I am creating a list of student objects
students = []
for data in json_data:
    student = Student(data['name'], int(data['grade']), int(data['testscore']), data['fav_subject'])
    students.append(student)
   # print(student.name) -- works! // gets students name
total_score = []
fav_students_total_score = []
not_fav_students_total_score = []
g9_total_score = []
g10_total_score = []
g11_total_score = []
g12_total_score = []
for student in students:
    total_score.append(student.testscore)
    if student.fav_subject == "yes":
        fav_students_total_score.append(student.testscore)
    else:
        not_fav_students_total_score.append(student.testscore)
    if student.grade == 9:
        g9_total_score.append(student.testscore)
    if student.grade == 10:
        g10_total_score.append(student.testscore)
    if student.grade == 11:
        g11_total_score.append(student.testscore)
    if student.grade == 12:
        g12_total_score.append(student.testscore)
   



#avg_stuff
avg_class_score = numpy.mean(total_score)
median_class_score = numpy.median(total_score)
print("The total number of students is:", len(students))
print("The total class mean was:", avg_class_score)
print("The total class median was:", median_class_score)
avg_fav_mean_score = numpy.mean(fav_students_total_score)
avg_not_fav_mean_score = numpy.mean(not_fav_students_total_score)
print("The average score for students whose favorite class was US History was:", avg_fav_mean_score)
print("The average score for students whose favorite class was not US History was:", avg_not_fav_mean_score)
avg_9_score = numpy.mean(g9_total_score)
avg_10_score = numpy.mean(g10_total_score)
avg_11_score = numpy.mean(g11_total_score)
avg_12_score = numpy.mean(g12_total_score)
print("The average score for freshmen was:", avg_9_score)
print("The average score for sophomores was:", avg_10_score)
print('The average score for juniors was:', avg_11_score)
print('The average score for seniors was:', avg_12_score)

Task 1: Analyzing Class Performance

For Task 1, we calculated the mean and median score of the midterm exam for all 25 students in the US History class. The results showed that the total class mean was 81.56%, while the total class median was 81%. This means that most students got a score that was close to 81%. Also, the median and the mean were both very similar, meaning that there were not many outliers within the class.

Task 2: Analyzing Students' Favorite Subjects

For Task 2, we calculated and compared the average scores of those who considered US History their favorite subject. The results showed that the average score for students whose favorite subject was US History was 95.375%, while the average score for students whose favorite subject was not US History was 75.05882352941178%; students who really enjoyed US History usually did better than those who did not. Additionally, students who listed US History as their favorite subject usually did much better than the class average, while students whose favorite subject was not US History did worse than the class average.

Task 3: Analyzing Performance by Grade

For Task 3, we calculated and compared the average scores of different grade levels who took the midterm exam. The results showed that the average score for freshmen was 80.4%, the average score for sophomores was 94.0%, the average score for juniors was about 78.1%, and the average score for seniors was 72.0%. The juniors and seniors did noticeably worse on the midterm than their younger peers, while the sophomores did much better than everyone else.

Conclusion:

In Task 1, we found that the class average was 81.56% and the class median was 81%. For Task 2, we found that the average score of students whose favorite subject was US History was higher than both the average score of students whose favorite subject was not US History and the class average. Also, the average score of students who did not list US History as their favorite subject was lower than the class average. In Task 3, we found that, on average, sophomores did the best in the exam, then freshmen, then juniors, and lastly seniors.
